Bio

I've had a lifelong obsession with making things using computers. I started learning to code because I was a video game fanatic as a kid, and wanted to make my own games.

When our family PC broke down and we couldn't afford another one, I started collecting scrap computers and parts other people threw away and built my own custom Linux boxes.

20 years later, I've worked at several small companies and startups including 2 of my own, one of which (YesInsights) was acquired in 2017. In that time, I've worked on:

  • A platform that used crowdsourced analysis to score audio on subjective criteria like humor, professionalism, and more (before AI was available to do so).
  • Manufacturing software that increased productivity on auto production lines, using a failsafe distributed cluster of servers deployed on-premises.
  • A publishing platform powered by a custom headless CMS, generating static pages from dynamic data (before headless CMSes were available off the shelf).

And countless other projects, prototypes, and experiments. In my spare time, I like to write short fiction, read sci-fi stories and history books, and play video games.
